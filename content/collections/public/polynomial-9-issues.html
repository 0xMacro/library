Protocol Design ack ## [I-1] Unsecured borrowing adds additional systemic risk to liquidity providers The [UnsecuredCreditModule](https://github.com/Polynomial-Protocol/fx-contracts/blob/435b19a3fddcb6cc7725644c4acbbd5ba4a2f664/protocol/synthetix/contracts/modules/core/UnsecuredCreditModule.sol#L18) allows markets to borrow without correlation to pool backing. Available credit is determined by [global and per-market caps](https://github.com/Polynomial-Protocol/fx-contracts/blob/435b19a3fddcb6cc7725644c4acbbd5ba4a2f664/protocol/synthetix/contracts/modules/core/UnsecuredCreditModule.sol#L153-L156), not pool collateral, but potential yield generated from these unsecured loans get distributed only to pools that back the market as it gets distributed via [distributeDebtToPools()](https://github.com/Polynomial-Protocol/fx-contracts/blob/435b19a3fddcb6cc7725644c4acbbd5ba4a2f664/protocol/synthetix/contracts/storage/Market.sol#L453-L478). This allows for unsecured markets to potentially borrow more than assets provided by backing pools, resulting in systemic risk for the entire protocol, not just backing pools if these assets are not paid off. Currently there is only one pool in the polynomial core protocol, which means all liquidity providers will be backing these unsecured markets, and take on the risk of default. If in the future more pools are added, allowing for the choice to back these unsecured markets, currently the systemic risk would still be present for these pools, yet they would not receive the proceeds. If more pools are added in the future, consider capping the credit limit of unsecured loans based on the assets pools, or implement bad debt to only effect backing pools to limit the risks to only the pools backing these markets. Unsecured borrowing adds additional systemic risk to liquidity providers Acknowledged. Unsecured credit is an opt‑in feature gated by owner controls and explicit caps (global + per‑market). Today there is a single pool, so all LPs implicitly back the system. As we expand to multi‑pool setups, we plan to scope unsecured exposure to backing pools only or cap unsecured credit based on pool‑provided collateral, so that non‑backing pools do not inherit the risk. This is a known design trade‑off and will be addressed alongside multi‑pool support.
