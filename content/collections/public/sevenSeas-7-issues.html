Informational medium ## [I-1] Addresses can be blacklisted from interacting with boringVault shares There is now the ability for an authorized role to update a `denyList` which prevents any address on the list from sending, receiving, or facilitating the transfer of vault shares. Although this is an accepted practice, and widely used tokens like USDC also have blacklist functionality, users should be aware that they can have their shares frozen. Blacklisting addresses should typically only be done to freeze assets of malicious users or prevent interaction with undesired contracts. Developers should also be aware that for contracts interacting with these shares, that either users or the contract itself can be blacklisted, which may adversely effect the contracts functionality.
